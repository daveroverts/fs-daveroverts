Apparently, it is not clear to everyone how AFV should be handled when using relief callsigns. This also applies when observing. If you only want the most important part, jump straight to the [summary](#summary).

## AFV database

The AFV database contains data such as callsigns, frequencies and transceivers. A transceiver can be seen as one or more linked antenna sites. For DEL, GND and TWR positions this is usually a single transceiver, but for APP and especially CTR positions there are often many more.

## AFV standalone client

When you are connected to VATSIM and then log in to the AFV standalone client, it automatically takes over the callsign and frequency you are logged in with. If you log in as a controller with a known callsign, AFV also loads the matching transceiver or transceivers. By default, both RX and TX are enabled for your own callsign.

However, when you log in with a relief callsign, such as `EHAM__W_APP`, AFV falls back to a transceiver based on your first visibility point. In the case of `EHAM_APP`, this means that traffic near EHRD and EHLE may have trouble hearing you.

The table below, from section 6.4.1.2 “Undefined Positions” of the [AFV manual](https://audio.vatsim.net/downloads/manual.pdf), shows why: the range is not that high.

![AFV default transceiver ranges for undefined positions](/static/images/2026-07-09-afv-and-relief-callsigns/afv-default-table.png)

## TrackAudio

TrackAudio handles this slightly differently. When you log in with a known callsign, it shows all callsigns that belong to that station. For example, when using `EHAM_W_APP`, you will also see stations such as `EHAM_E_APP` and `EHAM_A_APP`. You then need to enable TX yourself. RX is enabled automatically. For APP and CTR positions, it is also recommended to use XCA.

When you log in with a relief callsign, such as `EHAM__W_APP`, TrackAudio immediately warns you that the station is unknown. In that case, you should manually add `EHAM_W_APP` and listen in using RX. Once the frequency is fully yours, enable TX and XCA.

---

## Use case 1: taking over EHAM_W_APP using a relief callsign

### Audio for VATSIM

1. In EuroScope, log in with the relief callsign, for example `EHAM__W_APP`.
2. Start the Audio for VATSIM standalone client and log in.
3. RX is now enabled on your own callsign. Disable it.
4. Manually add `EHAM_W_APP` and enable **RX** to listen in.
5. When it is time to take over the frequency, first select `EHAM_W_APP` in EuroScope under **Voice Communication Setup** and click **Prim**.
6. In the audio client, enable **TX** and **XC(A)**.
7. That is it. You now have five transceivers, meaning aircraft near EHRD and EHLE should also be able to hear you.

### TrackAudio

1. In EuroScope, log in with the relief callsign, for example `EHAM__W_APP`.
2. Start TrackAudio and log in. TrackAudio detects that you are using a relief callsign and gives you two options: use the regular callsign (`EHAM_W_APP`) or the relief callsign (`EHAM__W_APP`). Be sure to select the regular callsign, `EHAM_W_APP`.
3. Because you selected the regular callsign, TrackAudio loads all matching transceivers and RX is enabled automatically.
4. When it is time to take over the frequency, select `EHAM_W_APP` in EuroScope under **Voice Communication Setup** and click **Prim**.
5. In TrackAudio, enable **TX** and **XCA**.

## Use case 2: observing EHAM_W_APP

### Audio for VATSIM

1. In EuroScope, log in as an observer. You may be tempted to click **Prim** on `EHAM_W_APP`, but that does not help in this case.
2. Start the Audio for VATSIM standalone client and log in.
3. **RX** is now enabled on your own callsign. Disable it.
4. Manually add `EHAM_W_APP` and enable RX to listen in.

### TrackAudio

1. In EuroScope, log in as an observer. You may be tempted to click **Prim** on `EHAM_W_APP`, but that does not help in this case.
2. Start TrackAudio and log in.
3. Click the **Add** button in the top-left.

   ![TrackAudio add button](/static/images/2026-07-09-afv-and-relief-callsigns/trackaudio-add-button.png)

4. Under **Add a Station**, type `EHAM_W_APP` and click **Add**.

   ![Adding EHAM_W_APP as a station in TrackAudio](/static/images/2026-07-09-afv-and-relief-callsigns/trackaudio-add-station.png)

5. You will now see all callsigns related to `EHAM_W_APP`. For `EHAM_W_APP`, click **RX**.

   ![Enabling RX on EHAM_W_APP in TrackAudio](/static/images/2026-07-09-afv-and-relief-callsigns/trackaudio-select-rx.png)

## Summary

When using a relief callsign, do not rely on the audio client automatically selecting the correct transceivers.

The important part is this:

1. Log in to EuroScope using the relief callsign.
2. In the audio client, disable RX on your own relief callsign if it was enabled automatically.
3. Manually add the real station callsign, for example `EHAM_W_APP`.
4. Use RX to listen in.
5. When taking over, select the real station in EuroScope, click **Prim**, then enable **TX** and **XC(A)** in the audio client.

For observing, the same principle applies: manually add the station you want to monitor and listen using **RX**.
